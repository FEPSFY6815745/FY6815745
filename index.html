Introduction

          The golden ratio, also known as the divine proportion, is a mathematical concept that has captivated the minds of scholars, artists, and nature enthusiasts for centuries. This ratio, 
          approximately 1.618, has been observed and admired in various elements of nature, from the delicate spirals of seashells to the intricate patterns of sunflowers (Mathai et al.,  1974). 
          In this article, we will explore the appearances of the golden ratio in nature, examining its prevalence in different organisms and its role in creating visually appealing and harmonious
